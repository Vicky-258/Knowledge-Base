# VDL-2: Vicky Design Language  
### _Clarity. Depth. Discipline. Character. Curiosity._

VDL-2 is the official, **product-agnostic** design language for all Vicky apps, tools, interfaces, and written systems.  
It does **not** belong to any specific product (portfolio, resume, app).  
Products simply **implement** VDL-2; the language itself stands independent.

VDL-2 represents a **curious, disciplined builder** who learns continuously, designs with intention, and expresses personality through subtle intelligenceâ€”not noise.

This document defines the **visual, tonal, and behavioral** rules of the design system.

---

## 1. CORE IDENTITY

### **Archetype:** The Curious Builder

- Smart but humble  
- Clean, factual communication  
- Humor exists only when natural  
- Driven by learning, not showing off  
- Solves problems calmly, without drama  
- Curious about everything  

**Tone summary:**  
> Calm, curious, precise. A UI that thinks before it speaks.

---

## 2. DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

### **2.1 Clarity**
- Every element must earn its place  
- No decoration without function  
- Minimal, but never empty  

### **2.2 Depth**
- Subtle gradients  
- Ultra-soft shadows  
- Atmospheric layers  
- Depth should feel like _potential energy_, not visual clutter  

### **2.3 Discipline**
- Small details matter  
- Restraint is a feature  
- Typography and spacing follow rules  

### **2.4 Character**
- Light humor  
- Gentle personality  
- Never cringe  
- Intelligent, observational, aware  

### **2.5 Curiosity**
- Reflects ongoing learning  
- Systems feel open-ended  
- Errors guide, not blame  

---

## 3. TYPOGRAPHY

### **Headings**
- Medium weight  
- Slightly tight tracking  
- Confident, not loud  

### **Body**
- Neutral weight  
- Higher line height  
- Optimized for readability  

### **Technical / Insight Text**
- Geist Mono or equivalent  
- Used for metadata, labels, and system cues  

### **Never Use**
- Ultra bold  
- Meme fonts  
- Excessive italics  
- Playful typefaces  

Typography must feel like:  
> â€œSomeone who cares about words and how they behave.â€
