## Key Concepts ðŸ”‘:

- Span: The set of all vectors you get when you scale two basis vectors.
- Basis: The set of vectors that when scaled gives you the span of the total vector space.
- Linearly Dependent: If a vector is equal or say lay on top of another vector in 2-d space and if the third vector happens to be in the span of the other two vectors say 
$$
\vec{w} = \vec{u} + \vec{v}$$
    then it is said to be linearly dependent
- Linearly independent: If all the vectors involved doesn't fall in any other's linear space or any two's span then they are said to be linearly independent.
